Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to educate college students about basic cybersecurity concepts and help them protect their personal information online. The site will provide clear explanations of common cyber threats, practical safety tips, and simple tools to help users improve their online security habits. By increasing awareness and understanding, the website aims to help users make safer decisions in their everyday digital lives.

Audience

The target audience for this website is college students, especially those with limited knowledge of cybersecurity. This includes students who regularly use the internet for school, social media, and online services but may not fully understand the risks of cyber threats. The site is designed for beginners who want simple, practical guidance on how to stay safe online.

JavaScript Usage

JavaScript will be used on the "Common Threats" page to create a dynamic, interactive experience. A dropdown menu will allow users to select a specific cybersecurity topic, and the page content will update with the relevant explanation, example, and safety tips.

Common Cyber Threats

Cyber threats are dangers that can harm your personal information, devices, or online accounts. Understanding these threats is the first step to staying safe online. Below are some of the most common risks students face and how to avoid them.

Phishing Attacks What it is: Phishing is when attackers try to trick you into giving away sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers. They often pretend to be trusted companies through emails, texts, or fake websites. Example: An email that looks like it's from your bank asking you to “verify your account.” How to stay safe: - Don't click suspicious links - Check the sender's email address carefully - Never share personal information through email

Malware What it is: Malware is harmful software that can infect your devices and steal your information. It often spreads through infected downloads, email attachments, or malicious websites. Example: Getting a virus from a fake software download that slows down your computer. How to stay safe: - Only download software from trusted sources - Keep your antivirus software updated - Don't open email attachments from unknown senders

Weak Passwords What it is: Using simple or reused passwords makes it easy for attackers to access your accounts. Example: Using "password123" or your birthday as a password. How to stay safe: - Use strong, unique passwords for each account - Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible - Use a password manager to keep track of your passwords

Public Wi-Fi Risks What it is: Using unsecured public Wi-Fi networks can expose your personal information to hackers. Example: Checking your bank account while connected to free Wi-Fi at a coffee shop. How to stay safe: - Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi - Use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) for added security - Turn off automatic Wi-Fi connections
